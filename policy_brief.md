# Executive Summary

This study evaluates the impact of a hypothetical education support program on learning outcomes among Primary 5 and Primary 6 pupils attending a government primary school in rural Uganda.

Using synthetic data and quasi-experimental impact evaluation methods, the analysis examined whether participation in the program improved pupil test scores relative to a comparison group.

Two causal inference approaches were applied:

- Difference-in-Differences (DiD)
- Propensity Score Matching with Difference-in-Differences (PSM + DiD)

Both approaches produced similar results, suggesting that the education support program generated meaningful improvements in pupil learning outcomes.

### Key Findings

| Method | Estimated Impact |
|----------|----------------:|
| Difference-in-Differences | 7.22 points |
| PSM + Difference-in-Differences | 7.52 points |

The consistency of results across methods suggests that program participants experienced test score improvements of approximately 7 to 8 points relative to comparable non-participants.

---

# Background

Improving foundational learning remains a key challenge across many rural primary schools in Sub-Saharan Africa. Limited learning materials, inadequate instructional support, and low pupil engagement can negatively affect academic achievement.

Education support programs often seek to address these constraints through interventions such as:

- Remedial instruction
- Teacher coaching
- Learning materials
- Attendance monitoring
- Academic support activities

Evidence on program effectiveness is essential for informing education policy and resource allocation decisions.

---

# Program Description

The hypothetical intervention evaluated in this study included:

- Remedial reading support
- Mathematics tutoring
- Teacher coaching
- Provision of learning materials
- Attendance monitoring

The program targeted Primary 5 and Primary 6 pupils.

The intended outcome was an improvement in academic performance as measured through standardized test scores.

---

# Evaluation Question

> What is the impact of the education support program on pupil learning outcomes?

The primary outcome assessed was pupil test scores.

---

# Evaluation Methodology

## Data

The analysis used a synthetic dataset consisting of:

- 240 pupils
- Treatment and comparison groups
- Baseline and endline observations
- Total observations: 480

## Analytical Approaches

### Difference-in-Differences (DiD)

The Difference-in-Differences method compares changes in outcomes over time between treatment and comparison groups.

### Propensity Score Matching (PSM)

Propensity Score Matching was used to improve comparability between groups based on:

- Gender
- Class level
- Attendance rate
- Study hours

### PSM + Difference-in-Differences

The matched sample was then analyzed using Difference-in-Differences to estimate program impact under improved baseline comparability.

---

# Covariate Balance Assessment

Before impact estimation, Propensity Score Matching was used to construct a comparison group with characteristics similar to those of participating pupils.

## Matched Sample Balance

| Covariate | Comparison Group | Treatment Group |
|------------|----------------:|----------------:|
| Attendance Rate (%) | 84.41 | 84.98 |
| Study Hours per Week | 6.09 | 6.09 |
| Average Test Score | 72.83 | 72.60 |

The balance assessment indicates that treatment and comparison pupils were highly similar after matching, strengthening the credibility of subsequent impact estimates.

---

# Results

## Difference-in-Differences

The Difference-in-Differences model estimated an average program impact of:

**7.22 points**

This indicates that participating pupils improved their test scores by approximately 7.22 points more than pupils in the comparison group.

## PSM + Difference-in-Differences

After improving comparability through matching, the estimated program impact was:

**7.52 points**

The close similarity between the two estimates suggests that observable baseline differences were not a major driver of the findings.

---

# Interpretation

The findings suggest that the education support program contributed positively to learning outcomes among participating pupils.

The consistency between the DiD and PSM + DiD estimates increases confidence in the robustness of the results.

In practical terms, the program was associated with an improvement of approximately 7 to 8 test-score points relative to comparable pupils who did not receive the intervention.

---

# Policy Implications

The findings highlight the potential value of targeted education support interventions in improving learning outcomes.

Programs that combine instructional support, learning resources, and pupil monitoring may contribute to measurable gains in academic performance.

Education stakeholders may consider:

1. Expanding remedial learning support.
2. Strengthening teacher coaching initiatives.
3. Increasing access to learning materials.
4. Using attendance monitoring systems to identify at-risk pupils.
5. Embedding impact evaluation into future education programs.

---

# Limitations

Several limitations should be considered when interpreting the findings:

- The dataset is synthetic and does not represent actual pupils or schools.
- Results are intended for educational and portfolio purposes.
- The analysis includes only observed characteristics used in the matching process.
- Real-world programs may be influenced by additional contextual factors not represented in the data.

---

# Recommendations

Based on the findings, future education interventions should:

- Prioritize support for foundational literacy and numeracy.
- Monitor attendance and learning outcomes regularly.
- Incorporate impact evaluation frameworks during program design.
- Use rigorous causal inference methods to assess effectiveness.
- Scale interventions that demonstrate measurable improvements in learning outcomes.

---

# Conclusion

This study demonstrates how causal inference methods can be used to evaluate education interventions.

Across multiple analytical approaches, the education support program was associated with improvements in pupil learning outcomes of approximately 7 to 8 test-score points.

The project highlights the value of combining Propensity Score Matching and Difference-in-Differences to strengthen impact evaluation and support evidence-based decision-making.
